This photograph, captured on Al Diwan Street in March 2014, powerfully illustrates the administrative mechanics of eviction leading up to the demolition of historic Msheireb. Pinned to a heavy, traditional wooden door—its deep red paint heavily faded and peeling—are two stark official notices indicating the impending disruption of basic utilities. One notice from the Doha Municipality announces upcoming "road maintenance," while the other, from the Department of Electricity Distribution, warns that the power supply to the premises will be interrupted for "essential work". These bureaucratic warnings functioned as a precursor to forced displacement, as municipal authorities frequently shut off power and water to pressure low-income expatriate tenants into vacating the area.​

Hanging informally beside these authoritative notices is a stark reminder of the human lives affected by these policies. Several pieces of worn, stained, and frayed clothing—including a heavily deteriorated rag, a striped black and white garment, and a bundle of faded plaid fabric—are draped over the door hinges and a thick, rusted metal spring spanning the entrance. This contrast visually encapsulates the clash between the state's aggressive modernization agenda and the precarious reality of the laborers residing in the slums.​​

As the Msheireb Downtown Doha project advanced, the complete erasure of these historic neighborhoods involved not just bulldozers, but the systematic withdrawal of urban infrastructure from the migrant communities who had inhabited the area for decades. This door serves as a poignant artifact of "The Erasure of Msheireb," bearing the exact notifications that signaled the end of the residents' tenancy and their subsequent push to the distant, unseen fringes of the city.
